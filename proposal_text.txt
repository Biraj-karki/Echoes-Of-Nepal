1.  Introduction & Problem Statement  
 
Tourism is one of Nepal’s strongest economic drivers, with millions of visitors attracted each 
year by its mountains, culture, and festivals. However, despite this potential, the tourism sector 
lacks a centralized, modern, and youth -friendly digital platform that integrates authentic travel 
content, business services, and safety features. Tourists often rely on fragmented sources of 
information, which makes it difficult to plan trips with confidence. On the other hand, local 
businesses such as homestays and guides struggle to reach travelers effectively due to limited 
online visibility.  
 
There is no single integrated platform in Nepal that combines community -driven content, AI -
powered recommendations, vendor dashboards, and emergency support, which restricts the 
growth of the tourism industry and reduces the quality of traveler experiences.

 3. Project Goals  
 
The endeavor behind Echoes of Nepal is to develop a tourism solution that is not only smart and 
community -centric but also focused on elevating the experience of travel while enhancing safety 
and creating business avenues for community traders. The initiative attempts to resolve the core 
issues faced by travelers, small business owners, and regulation authorities and to make the 
solution theoretically profound and practically useful.

 6. Background of the Study  
 
On a global scale, travelers have experienced a paradigm shift in planning with the advent of 
tourism platforms such as TripAdvisor, Booking.com, and Google Maps. Despite their 
widespread usability, these platforms offer little to no insight on the unique cultures of different 
regions, nor do they address safety concerns and offline accessibility, which is of great 
importance in a country like Nepal that is deeply cherished for its off -the-beaten -path trekking 
and world heritage trails.  
 
Set against the backdrop of Nep al, numerous small -scale operators in the tourism sector make 
use of outdated websites or bland old -fashioned word of mouth to draw in tourists. This 
perpetuates a form of digital inequality where only the big hotels and international tourism 
companies hav e the luxury to be seen. It has previously been established that the involvement 
of local vendors, the use of smart technology, and the knowledge of the community can be of 
immense benefit to tourism. The construction of an AI -driven, localized, and safety -focused 
platform can thus aid in a more practical as well as academic sense.

 7. Functional Decomposition Diagram (FDD) and Functionalities  
 
 
Figure 1:Functional Decomposition Diagram  
In an effort to break down the proposed syste m into manageable units, a Functional 
Decomposition Diagram (FDD) is used. This diagram divides the platform into core modules and 
their respective sub -functions, making it easier to visualize and model the system. At the 
topmost level, Echoes of Nepal is structured into three major modules: the User Module, the 
Vendor Module, and the Admin Module.  
 
The User Module addresses the needs of travellers and young adventurers who engage with 
the platform. After registering and logging in to their accounts, users can compile their profiles 
and share travel narratives, photographs, and cultural insights. Users can also obtain AI -driven 
travel suggestions, which take into account their interests, budgets, and the appropriate 
seasons, thus enhancing the personalizatio n of their travels. In addition, users have the ability to 
engage with interactive smart maps, download offline guides and QR -based information, and 
activate the SOS feature in emergencies. Collectively, these functionalities provide users with 
tools that are rich in information and focus on their safety during travels.

 The Vendor Module is intended for local guides, homestay providers, and trekking agencies. 
Once verified, vendors can create and update their business profiles, publish travel package s. 
This module also provides a dashboard with key performance indicators, allowing vendors to 
monitor the performance of their offers, track popular destinations, and see user engagement 
with their content. The Vendor Module provides valuable tools that he lp small enterprises 
engage with travellers in a digital -first manner and make informed business decisions based on 
real data.  
The Admin Module is responsible for maintaining the integrity and credibility of the system. 
Admins oversee the entire platform b y verifying vendor registrations, approving packages, and 
ensuring authenticity in content. They also monitor SOS alerts raised by travelers, coordinating 
potential responses to emergencies. Additionally, admins are able to generate real -time tourism 
repor ts, which can be used by government bodies or agencies to track trends and improve 
policies. Thus, the Admin Module ensures not just supervision but also strategic guidance for 
the tourism industry.

 8. Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) Overview  
 
The development of Echoes of Nepal will adopt the Agile Software Development Life Cycle 
(SDLC) methodology. Agile is chosen for its flexibility, iterative nature, and focus on continuous 
feedback — qualities that are important for balancing technical requireme nts with practical user 
needs.  
In the Requirement Gathering Phase, the project will consider the perspectives of travelers, 
local vendors, and administrators to identify key features such as AI recommendations, maps, 
and SOS alerts. The System Design Phase  will then focus on creating wireframes, architecture 
diagrams, and database schemas, ensuring the system’s front -end and back -end elements 
integrate seamlessly. During the Implementation Phase, the team will work in iterative sprints to 
build modules incl uding authentication, content posting, vendor dashboards, and AI 
recommendations.  
The Testing Phase will include unit, integration, and system testing, as well as User Acceptance 
Testing (UAT) with selected users like students or local vendors to validate usability. The 
Deployment Phase will see the application hosted on cloud platforms with automated 
deployment pipelines. Finally, the Maintenance Phase will handle feedback, updates, and 
scaling, ensuring the system evolves alongside user expectations.

9. Technology Stack  
 
 
The technology stack for Echoes of Nepal has been carefully selected for scalability, 
performance, and security. The Frontend will be developed using Next.js, a React -based 
framework that allows server -side rendering, responsive inter faces, and offline PWA support. 
The Backend will be powered by Node.js with Express, ensuring fast and scalable handling of 
API requests.  
The Database may employ either MongoDB for flexible document -based storage or PostgreSQL 
for structured relational dat a, depending on the type of information handled. Authentication and 
Security will be implemented using JWT or Google OAuth, ensuring secure access control for 
users and vendors. Maps Integration will rely on Mapbox or Google Maps to provide detailed 
naviga tion and cultural overlays.  
For the AI Engine, the platform will use either the OpenAI API or custom -trained ML models to 
recommend destinations and experiences. Notifications will be sent using services like Twilio or 
MSG91, ensuring reliable delivery of SOS alerts and updates. Offline functionality will be 
achieved through PWA caching and QR -based guides. This stack is both modern and industry -
relevant, allowing the project to demonstrate practical application of widely adopted tools.

 11. Testing Meth odologies  
 
Proper testing procedures will have to be followed to complete the project in a manner such that 
the completed work is reliable, secure, and user -friendly. Proper unit testing will check individual 
functionalities such as logging in, posting sto ries, and SOS alerts. Integration testing will be 
done to check the integration of different modules, ensuring there is smooth cooperation 
between the frontend, backend, and the database. System testing will be conducted to verify 
that complete workflows —from user registration to vendor package booking —function correctly.  
 
A select group of travellers and vendors will be invited to test the platform in real time in order to 
conduct the User Acceptance Testing (UAT) phase, which focuses on usability feedbac k. In 
addition, Performance Testing will ascertain how well the system copes with simultaneous 
users, with special focus on peak travel periods. Security Testing will be conducted to find and 
fix any weaknesses such as unauthorized access or injection atta cks, especially considering the 
sensitive vendor and user data. The combination of these procedures helps guarantee that the 
platform is reliable both from a technical standpoint and in the trust of the users.

 12. Limitations  
 
Although Echoes of Nepal provides a comprehensive platform, some limitations are recognized. 
The SOS system will require strong coordination with local authorities and rescue teams, 
without which it may not achieve its full effectiveness. The AI recommendation engine will 
initiall y rely on limited datasets until sufficient community -generated content is collected, which 
may affect the accuracy of early suggestions. Offline access will also be limited to downloadable 
PDFs and QR codes in the first phase.  
 
Another limitation is scala bility: while the project will be able to handle a reasonable number of 
users, sudden growth in user base — such as during peak tourist seasons — may require 
additional investment in cloud infrastructure. Despite these limitations, the project provides a 
strong foundation that can be expanded in future iterations to include multilingual support 
beyond English and Nepali, AR/VR experiences, and mobile -native applications.
